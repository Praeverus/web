The Problem

Jury research is a luxury. It shouldn't be.

For decades, learning how jurors will react to your case meant a six- or seven-figure engagement — and it almost always came too late.

Traditional Focus Groups

Rare, late, and expensive

  • Reserved for only the most strategic cases — the overwhelming majority of matters are never tested at all.
  • Typically performed late in a dispute, after millions of dollars have already been spent on an untested theory.
  • By the time the results arrive, the case strategy is already locked in.
With Praeverus

Anytime. Any stage.

  • Run a focus group in hours, not months — before the complaint is filed, before a key deposition, before mediation, before trial.
  • Test every theory, not just the one you can afford to test once.
  • Test early. Test often. Refine strategy while it can still change the outcome.

Praeverus inverts the economics of jury research — so insight arrives before the strategy is set, not after the money is spent.

The Full-Information Advantage

Your synthetic jurors see everything.

In traditional focus groups and shadow juries, protective orders frequently prevent the most important evidence from ever being shown to research participants. Teams end up testing a sanitized version of their case — and learning how jurors react to a case that isn't theirs.

Traditional focus group · shadow jury

Protective orders keep key evidence out of the room. The research tests a redacted case.

Praeverus synthetic jurors

All the information. Learn how the real case — including its most sensitive evidence — will actually land.

And information flows both ways. Live focus group participants often won't disclose what they truly feel — telling researchers what sounds acceptable in the room, then deciding differently in private. It's the same dynamic that has made election polling famously miss what voters actually do. Synthetic jurors have no one to impress: you get the candid reaction, not the polite one.

Objective by Design

How jurors actually decide.

Praeverus is objective — grounded in data rather than a consultant's intuition. It models how specific traits genuinely influence human decision-making, and how those individuals influence each other.

Trait-level modeling

Background, values, attitudes, and case-relevant experience — modeled for how each trait actually shapes verdict-relevant judgments, not how conventional wisdom says it should.

Deliberation dynamics

Praeverus models the deliberation room itself: how strong personalities persuade, how coalitions form, and how a single skeptical juror can shift the entire panel.

Beyond individual verdicts

An individual prediction is a data point. A modeled deliberation is a strategy — showing you not just where jurors start, but where the room ends up.
